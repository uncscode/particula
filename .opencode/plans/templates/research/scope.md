### Purpose
Define strict in-scope and out-of-scope boundaries so the research remains executable, reviewable, and resistant to scope drift.

### Required-content checklist
- [ ] In-scope experiments, systems, and datasets are listed.
- [ ] Out-of-scope items are explicitly documented.
- [ ] Time and resource boundaries are stated.
- [ ] Interfaces to adjacent workstreams are identified.

### Drafter prompts
- Which variables are being varied versus held constant?
- What tempting extensions are intentionally deferred?
- What upstream/downstream teams must be informed of scope limits?

### Example
In scope: ablation of prompt-template strategies across three benchmark tasks on the existing training corpus. Out of scope: model architecture changes, new data acquisition, and production deployment automation.
